# Student Archetype

**Who they are:** A self-taught practitioner who came to GTM through curiosity rather than credential. They are avid, capable, and more technically literate than the job market currently recognizes. They have held SDR, AE, RevOps, or marketing ops roles — or are transitioning from a technical field into GTM. They are not a student who wants to pass a certification; they are a practitioner who wants to build revenue infrastructure that works while they sleep.

**What they already know:** Basic familiarity with at least one GTM tool (Clay, Apollo, HubSpot, or Salesforce). Comfortable with spreadsheets and business logic. May have written Python scripts or SQL queries but has not built production systems. Has seen the GTM automation hype cycle and is skeptical of tool-first training that doesn't explain why anything works.

**What they need:** A technical foundation that anchors every GTM tool claim in a mechanism they can verify. Not "use Clay for enrichment" — but "here is the FETE loop, here is the enrichment waterfall, here is the API endpoint, here is why the waterfall fallback order matters." The ability to reason about GTM systems from first principles so that when the tool stack rotates (it will), they can adapt rather than recertify.

**End goal:** A working GTM engineer who owns the full revenue stack. Signal detection → ICP scoring → copy at scale → production GTM infrastructure → autonomous agent systems. Deployable as a $10K/month retainer operator, an in-house GTM engineer, or a founder building their own pipeline. Optionality to cross into AI engineering if the GTM market corrects. Job title: `.engineer`.
